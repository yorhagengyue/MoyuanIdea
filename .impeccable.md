## Design Context

### Users
- Primary learners are children aged 4-8 in AI-assisted cultural classes.
- A teacher or demo presenter controls the flow on a big screen.
- Parents often observe outcomes and care about clear evidence of progress.
- The core classroom job: understand a concept, make a choice, explain why, and get immediate guidance.

### Brand Personality
- Three-word personality: warm, trustworthy, evidence-based.
- Tone: encouraging but specific, never vague praise.
- Emotional goal: children feel safe to speak, curious to try again, and proud after improvement.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Direction: hand-painted watercolor classroom.
- Visual mood: paper texture, soft pigments, playful but organized composition.
- Must align with provided mountain sketch reference for educational familiarity.
- Hard constraints:
  - No cheap icon-style choice cards.
  - Choice cards must use real generated images.
  - Avoid generic AI template aesthetics and decorative gimmicks.

### Design Principles
1. Learning clarity first: every section explains what to do now.
2. Child-safe feedback: identify right/wrong points with gentle guidance.
3. Evidence over noise: feedback must show judgement, issue, corrected sentence, and next step.
4. Visual friendliness with control: expressive style, minimal controls, no interaction clutter.
5. Production-ready reliability: loading and failure states never break classroom flow.
